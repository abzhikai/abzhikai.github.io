About Manga

Manga is a term used in Japan to refer to comics or graphic novels. Outside of the country the word is typically used to refer to comics originally published in the country.


Manga stories are usually printed in black and white due to time constraints, artistic reasons and to keep printing prices low. In Japan, manga are usually serialized in large manga magazines which contain many stories, each having one episode of the stories to be continued in the next issue.


Most manga conform to a style developed in Japan in the late 19th century and the form has a long history in earlier Japanese art.


Manga has many different genres and there are targeted audiences.

Shonen - Manga targeted at boys who are tweens and teens

Shojo - Manga targeted at girls who are tweens and teens

Seinen - Manga targeted at adult men

Josei - Manga targeted at adult women

Kodomomuke - Manga targeted at young children

Manga in Comparison to Light Novels and Anime

Some manga are adapted from the stories of Light Novels and turned into Anime. In comparison to its story telling counterparts, when it comes to telling the story, Light Novels usually have the full uncut plot of the story written down and as for anime, it has the best visual representation of the story. Manga is the center with a mix of both, having a good mix of each.
